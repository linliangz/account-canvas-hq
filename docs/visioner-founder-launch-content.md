## LinkedIn Post 1: Problem Framing

Most CRMs were not designed for the person actually working a strategic account.

They are great for records, stages, dashboards, and forecasts.

But a Key Account Manager's daily questions are different:

- Which stakeholder has gone cold?
- Which project needs attention today?
- Do I actually know the economic buyer?
- Who reports to whom?
- What changed in this account since last week?
- What should I do next?

That is the gap Visioner is designed around: an Account Planning CRM for Key Account Managers.

Not another reporting database. A daily workspace for account plans, projects, stakeholders, tasks, relationship health, and account signals.

I am opening the first version for KAMs and founders managing 3-5 high-value accounts.

Start here: https://www.visioner.cc/

## LinkedIn Post 2: Account Plan Angle

Account plans should not live only in slides.

A useful account plan should answer:

- Where are we against target?
- Which projects are active?
- Who influences each project?
- What relationship is weak?
- What stakeholder is missing?
- What is the next action?

The problem is that most account plan templates are created for reviews, not daily work.

Visioner turns the account plan into a working canvas: revenue, projects, stakeholders, org chart, tasks, notes, email activity, and signals stay connected.

The first version is SaaS-first and free for three accounts.

Guide: https://www.visioner.cc/guides/what-should-an-account-plan-include

## LinkedIn Post 3: Stakeholder Mapping Angle

In large accounts, the deal rarely dies because one field was missing in CRM.

It dies because the real decision chain was never mapped.

Common gaps:

- Champion is friendly but not senior enough
- Economic buyer is unknown
- Security or IT gets involved too late
- Procurement is treated as admin, not a stakeholder
- A new VP arrives and nobody notices
- The account owner only talks to the same 5-10 contacts

Visioner makes stakeholder mapping a core part of account planning, not an optional attachment.

Org chart, decision roles, relationship health, unknown seats, and next actions all live together.

Guide: https://www.visioner.cc/guides/how-to-map-stakeholders-in-a-strategic-account

## LinkedIn Post 4: Daily Workflow Story

A typical day for a Key Account Manager should not start with "update CRM."

It should start with:

1. Check portfolio and target gap
2. Review today's urgent and important tasks
3. Open the project that actually needs attention
4. Check stakeholder coverage and relationship health
5. Send the follow-up from normal email
6. BCC Visioner so activity logs itself
7. Improve the account map over time
8. End the week with progress already captured

That is the product philosophy behind Visioner.

CRM should become a byproduct of doing the work, not a separate chore after the work.

Try it with three accounts: https://www.visioner.cc/

## LinkedIn Post 5: Account Mapping Angle

Account mapping is not a contact list.

A useful account map shows:

- who reports to whom
- which stakeholder roles matter for each project
- where relationship health is strong or weak
- which seats are still unknown
- what the next action should be

For Key Account Managers, this matters because large accounts are not won by updating more CRM fields. They are won by understanding how the customer actually works.

Visioner is built around that daily account map.
